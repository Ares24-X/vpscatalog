Detailed Analysis

Affiliate marketers usually make the mistake of sorting hosting programs by headline payout. That is too shallow. A $200 commission is only valuable if the visitor can sign up without heavy friction, the provider approves the conversion, and the keyword has buyer intent. A lower commission can beat a higher one when the host has better pricing, stronger brand trust, or a longer cookie window.

DigitalOcean is the cleanest arithmetic play in this set. A developer can start with a small Droplet, the brand has high trust, and the first-time bounty creates a large positive spread. This makes DigitalOcean a strong target for keywords such as “best VPS for developers,” “DigitalOcean vs Vultr,” and “cheap cloud server for WordPress.” Read our DigitalOcean review and the DigitalOcean vs Vultr vs Linode comparison.

Linode / Akamai is also attractive because the 90-day cookie gives content more time to convert. That matters for comparison searches where buyers read several pages before deciding. The $100 qualified referral can produce a positive spread even on a basic annual plan. Read the Linode review.

Cloudways is a different game. The cheapest annual cost is higher because you are selling managed hosting, not bare VPS. The initial spread is slightly negative against the base commission, but managed WordPress traffic can be more valuable because the buyer is often a business owner rather than a tinkerer. If volume tiers push commission above $125, Cloudways becomes much more interesting. See Cloudways vs SiteGround and our Cloudways review.

Vultr is a good host but a weaker affiliate arbitrage candidate. The product converts for global VPS keywords, but the payout is lower than the annual cost of the practical $6/month entry plan. Use Vultr to make comparison pages credible, then route users toward higher-spread choices when appropriate. Read the Vultr review.

Hetzner is break-even on published numbers. It is still worth covering because EU performance and price-to-spec keywords bring serious buyers. The monetization strategy is volume, email capture, and cross-linking to higher-spread programs.

RackNerd and Contabo should remain in the editorial mix because they win budget and spec-heavy searches. But without public affiliate programs, they are not the first choice for paid traffic or aggressive arbitrage.

Verdict

If you are building a hosting review blog for affiliate income, start with DigitalOcean, Linode, Cloudways, SiteGround, Bluehost, Hostinger, InterServer, and ScalaHosting pages. Keep Vultr, Hetzner, RackNerd, and Contabo in comparisons to earn trust and capture long-tail searches.
